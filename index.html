The Curator

Ivan Suprun

Over fifteen years — image as a whole.

Ivan never built an image in pieces. From the very beginning he was drawn to the whole — to how a person looks from head to toe, and the impression they make walking into a room. Even at school he was the one people came to before an occasion that mattered: assembling looks for friends' proms, choosing outfits, and staging entire events — from choreography and décor to costumes and casting. He always had his own vision of how things ought to be, and it never failed to leave an impression.

His craft grew out of that sense of the whole. He didn't learn it in sequence but developed it in parallel — clothing, hair, make-up — because an image only comes alive when all of it speaks together. At a certain point it converged on its own: he became the master people came to for hair and make-up, and left with a complete image.

His sense of image is cinematic. Trained as a director and producer, he worked as lead producer on more than fifty music videos, responsible for the entire production — the discipline of positioning a person, composing an impression, and making status legible on camera. It is the same instinct he now brings to private clients.

It began organically: clients came for hair and make-up, then asked him to assemble the whole look. Capsules, shopping, wardrobe edits followed — and it became clear this was where he was truly singular. Among the first were top Ukrainian models, then the wives of influential figures, then the men themselves.

His practice has always been closed, and to this day it grows by personal introduction alone — a new client arrives through someone already within it. It is a private circle more than a client list.

Ivan does not work like a stylist who makes people look expensive. He positions a person in a room — the way a strategist positions a reputation. He is against the logic that costlier means better; his fee pays for itself precisely because everything acquired earns its place and stays in the wardrobe, rather than being bought once and never worn.

Who I Work With

Clients

Means without a system, given order.

Over the years Ivan has worked with very different people, united by one thing: means without a system. They spend a great deal, often wrongly, buying the expensive as proof of status — and achieving the opposite. What Ivan gives is the one thing money can't: a trained eye and order.

One client came by personal introduction while still an executive. Over the years she grew — in confidence, in contacts, in reputation — and founded her own company, her image evolving with her. In time the work became so continuous that it moved to a monthly arrangement: not occasional sessions, but standing access to Ivan.

Another is a lawyer who, twice a year, goes through a shopping day and a full wardrobe edit with Ivan, where new pieces are built into what already exists. His purchases had been impulsive — expensive things bought for the name on the label, impossible to wear. Now each one earns its place.

A third is a private client Ivan guides entirely: not only wardrobe and dressing for occasions, but hair, make-up, and care — everything an image is made of. Much of it happens remotely; in person when she visits. She came without knowing how or for what to dress, and found clarity, and a quiet confidence in every appearance.

He is singular, and clients say so themselves — they have never had an experience like it.

Every engagement begins with a consultation, and everything follows from it — built individually around the person, their rhythm, and what they need. There is no fixed script; each client's path is composed for them.
